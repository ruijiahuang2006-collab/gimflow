### G. Exploratory Split of Post-Experiment Scores by Second-Session Condition

Reported for descriptive completeness only. Not interpreted as a condition-specific comparison, because each participant had experienced both conditions before completing SUS and TX. Small subgroup sizes preclude inferential testing.

In both studies, participants who experienced the GIMFlow condition as their second session gave lower descriptive post-experiment scores than participants who experienced the Baseline condition as their second session. Because SUS and TX are single post-experiment measures administered once after both conditions have been experienced, this pattern cannot be interpreted as a condition-specific preference and is more consistent with recency or ordering effects than with a condition-level judgment. Primary condition-focused interpretation relies on the paired pre/post SAM and PANAS analyses (§5.1, §5.3), the direction consistency across studies (§5.3), and the blinded pairwise LLM analysis (§5).
